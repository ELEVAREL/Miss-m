1. Introduction

Consumer behaviour has long been recognised as a cornerstone of effective marketing strategy. In an increasingly fragmented marketplace, understanding how and why consumers make purchasing decisions enables organisations to develop targeted campaigns that resonate deeply with their intended audience.

This essay examines Nike's application of the Segmentation, Targeting and Positioning (STP) framework within the global sportswear market. By analysing Nike's strategic decisions across demographic, psychographic and behavioural dimensions, this paper argues that its segmentation approach has been central to sustaining brand dominance across diverse consumer cohorts (Kotler & Keller, 2016).

Furthermore, this analysis will draw upon Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs to contextualise Nike's positioning strategy within broader theories of consumer motivation, before presenting recommendations for how these approaches might evolve in response to shifting digital consumption patterns.
